During the early weeks of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, I wrote two op-eds published in the **Atlanta Journal-Constitution** and organized a grassroots campaign in my home state of Georgia.

## Op-Eds

- **[Opinion: Shut Ga. down now to save lives, economy](https://www.ajc.com/news/opinion/opinion-shut-down-now-save-lives-economy/SnKDZg8MDXWiZfsegHAxHO)** *(with Akshat Pujara and Mike Callahan)* — Published shortly before Georgia's April 1st shutdown. I compared Georgia's trajectory to Italy's and made the case that economic and healthcare interests were aligned.

- **[Opinion: Wearing masks must become mandatory](https://www.ajc.com/news/opinion/opinion-wearing-masks-must-become-mandatory/CuAkakHXNcEnCD0xTABMrJ/)** — Written in mid-May and published in July as cases surged. Two weeks after publication, Governor Kemp sued Atlanta Mayor Bottoms to overturn her city-wide mask mandate.

## #ShutdownGA Petition

I created a [Change.org petition](https://www.change.org/p/tell-governor-kemp-shut-down-georgia-today-save-lives-shutdownga) calling on Governor Kemp to shut down Georgia, which gathered **80,000+ signatures** in two weeks. I sent it to my governor and federal representatives, performed Georgia-specific hospital capacity analysis, and emailed it to over 200 state legislators and city mayors. I also built a [coordination website](https://sites.google.com/view/shutdownga/) for volunteers.
